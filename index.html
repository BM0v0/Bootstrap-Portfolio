About Me

Brandon Moran is an aspiring audio engineer that has an ever expanding interest with almost all things relating to audio. He started developing his skills in the Music Industry program at Diablo Valley Community College(DVC) in 2011. There he would learn about studio opperations and the functionallity of several DAW's (Digital Audio Workstation). He achieved his User certification with Pro Tools and completed his certification of completion in Music Industry Studies at DVC. During this time he also volunteered for the 137th Audio Engineering Society convention in LA in 2014 were he met his idol, Martin O'Donnell who composed the music for the video game Halo that inspired him to get into the game audio industry. Later he was accepted into San Francisco State University in 2015 under the Broadcasting & Electronic Communication Arts (BECA) department. During his time there he took on a few Live Sound gigs under the guidence of one of his mentors Rajeev Naidu. He also worked as a stage hand for the company LiveNation. He then built his first "monster" computer to expand his capabilities in the realm of audio and gained a lot of exposure to the live sound industry when he covered shows at the Shoreling Ampitheatre & SAP Cneter for Muse, Boston & Joan Jett, Bruno Mars, Enrique Iglesias & PitBull, etc..

He also gained exposure from the Radio Industry as an intern for The Chip Franklin Show on KGO 810 Cumulus Media. He also interned for Polarity Post production studios where he would understand the processes of handling clients for advertisements, audio books and films. While at SF state, he took on the posistion of President for the Audio Engineering Society(AES) student organization during 2017. During this time he won the nomination for "Best Sound Design" in the BECA department at SF state. He later graduated in 2017 and is currently the sound mixer/composer for 2 upper graduate thesis films set to premier May 23, 2018.
